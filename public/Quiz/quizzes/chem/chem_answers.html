Chemistry Quiz - Correct Answers

1. What is the atomic number of Oxygen?

Correct Answer: 8

Explanation: The atomic number of Oxygen is 8, which means it has 8 protons in its nucleus.

2. What is the molecular formula of water?

Correct Answer: H2O

Explanation: Water is composed of two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom, giving it the molecular formula H2O.

3. What is the most abundant gas in Earth's atmosphere?

Correct Answer: Nitrogen

Explanation: Nitrogen (N2) makes up about 78% of the Earth's atmosphere, making it the most abundant gas.

4. What is the pH of pure water?

Correct Answer: 7

Explanation: Pure water has a neutral pH of 7, meaning it is neither acidic nor basic.

5. Which element has the chemical symbol Na?

Correct Answer: Sodium

Explanation: Sodium (Na) is a chemical element with the symbol Na, which comes from the Latin word 'Natrium'.

6. What is the chemical formula for methane?

Correct Answer: CH4

Explanation: Methane (CH4) consists of one carbon atom bonded to four hydrogen atoms.

7. Which of the following is a noble gas?

Correct Answer: Neon

Explanation: Neon (Ne) is a noble gas, part of the inert gases group, known for its lack of chemical reactivity.

8. What is the chemical symbol for Gold?

Correct Answer: Au

Explanation: Gold has the chemical symbol Au, derived from the Latin word 'Aurum'.

9. Which of the following acids is found in the stomach?

Correct Answer: Hydrochloric acid

Explanation: Hydrochloric acid (HCl) is the strong acid found in the stomach, aiding in digestion and killing harmful microbes.

10. What is the atomic number of Carbon?

Correct Answer: 6

Explanation: The atomic number of Carbon is 6, which means it has 6 protons in its nucleus and forms the basis for organic life.
