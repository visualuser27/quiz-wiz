Chemistry Quiz: Answers and Explanations

Review your answers and learn more about each question!

Question 1

Q: What is the atomic number of oxygen?

Answer: 8

Explanation: The atomic number of oxygen is 8, which indicates it has 8 protons in its nucleus.

Question 2

Q: What is the chemical symbol for Sodium?

Answer: Na

Explanation: The symbol Na comes from the Latin name "Natrium" for Sodium.

Question 3

Q: What is the pH of pure water at room temperature?

Answer: 7

Explanation: Pure water is neutral, with a pH of 7, which means it has an equal concentration of H⁺ and OH⁻ ions.

Question 4

Q: What is the chemical formula of methane?

Answer: CH₄

Explanation: Methane consists of one carbon atom bonded to four hydrogen atoms.

Question 5

Q: What is the most abundant gas in the Earth's atmosphere?

Answer: Nitrogen

Explanation: Nitrogen makes up about 78% of the Earth's atmosphere by volume.

Question 6

Q: What is the process of converting a solid directly into a gas called?

Answer: Sublimation

Explanation: Sublimation is the process where a substance transitions directly from the solid phase to the gas phase without passing through the liquid phase.

Question 7

Q: What is Avogadro's number?

Answer: 6.022 × 10²³

Explanation: Avogadro's number represents the number of particles (atoms, molecules) in one mole of a substance.

Question 8

Q: What is the molar mass of water (H₂O)?

Answer: 18 g/mol

Explanation: Water has a molar mass of 18 g/mol, calculated as (2 × 1 for H) + (16 for O).

Question 9

Q: What is the common name for H₂SO₄?

Answer: Sulfuric Acid

Explanation: H₂SO₄ is known as sulfuric acid, a strong acid widely used in industry.

Question 10

Q: What is the state of matter of water at 0°C under normal atmospheric pressure?

Answer: Solid

Explanation: At 0°C and normal pressure, water freezes into its solid form, ice.
